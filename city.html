Rexburg Idaho was founded in 1883 by a group of pioneers who had been sent by Brigham Young. The new community, named Rexburg after their leader (Ricks being German for Rex or King) quickly attracted men and women eager to bring industry and civilization to the West. By the time Idaho entered the Union as the 43rd state in 1890, Rexburg was the second largest city in the state.


One of the most prominent features about Rexburg are both the university and the large potato culture and farmland in the area. Brigham Young University Idaho is one of the largest growing universities in the nation and continues to see a large increase in student enrollment. Since 2010, student enrollment has almost doubled, going from 12,500 students to almost 21,000 current students. Online enrollment has also increased and made easier as COVID has gone rampant.

Idaho in general is the land of potatoes, and Rexburg is no exception. The area is filled with acres and acres of farmland, ranging from cattle ranches to potato farms. When spud season comes along, many people come out to get the work done as quickly as possible.

During the summer times, there are various places to visit and enjoy. One of the local favorites is Warm Slough, a lazy river where people can go tubing, kayaking or just enjoy a day in the water. If a natural river is not attractive, the local water park offers fun at a good price.
